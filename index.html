Look 1

The first look explores fragmentation through asymmetry, layering, and contrasting knitted textures. The interaction between soft crochet, transparent mesh, and distorted proportions reflects the uneven and constantly shifting surfaces of the urban environment.

The second look focuses on tension, distortion, and the relationship between the body and material. Stretched tights form a fragile surface around the silhouette, reflecting both physical pressure and the instability associated with a constantly transforming urban space.

The third look explores reconstruction through layered garments and displaced elements of classical clothing. The combination of tailoring, transparent surfaces, and fragmented construction reflects the city as a structure continuously dismantled and reassembled.

The fourth look translates urban fragmentation into knitted and pleated structures. Exposed seams, separate knitted sections, and the architectural rhythm of pleating create a silhouette that appears simultaneously constructed, unfinished, and in a state of transformation.

The fifth look combines metallic surfaces with controlled deformation and a simplified elongated silhouette. The contrast between the silver knitted dress and the black outer layer refers to industrial materials, compressed structures, and the changing physical surfaces of the city.

The final look explores urban texture through draping, irregular volume, and the natural deformation of fabric. Its fragmented folds and unfinished visual rhythm reflect the city as a tactile landscape shaped by continuous processes of decay, reconstruction, and renewal.
